Saturno Rebranding

Shortly after arriving at Saturno I was asked to rebrand the company. I aimed for a few years behind the cutting edge of design, as our clients were law firms, not start ups, and wanted something a little more tried and true.


The orange was part of the original brand which I kept. They also had blue in the original brand, which I kept, but dialed back quite a lot. It can be seen in the vertical line going through the letterhead.


While I begged to give the entire website a makeover, we never found the time. However, I did get a chance to redesign the slides of the hero banner.

Ford Harrison Holiday Card

This was an annual holiday card that the law firm Ford Harrison sent out to all it's clients. Their clients were diverse so this card was meant to be strictly non-denominational.


It was designed for Outlook 2007 or later. The client would recieve an email asking to contribute a digital card, by saying their name and what they were thankful for. The email also specified that for each card, Ford Harrison would donate $75 to an orphanage in Cambodia that they work closely with.


They would be directed to a landing page with a modal open for them to put in their information.


Once they entered their information they were directed to a mural where they could see their card and everyone elses, including cards done by Ford Harrison's attorneys, that included photos. This helped humanize the firm and the attorneys in the eyes of the clients. It was also a bit absurd to design this in August!

Cohne Kinghorn Website

This is a fully responsive website for a Utah law firm called Cohne Kinghorn. Originally we only were supposed to redesign the hero banner and maybe the fonts/colors, as they were a low budget job. However, that week I had a lot of spare time, and I had been itching to try a split screen layout, as multiple graphic design blogs mentioned they were gaining popularity.


I turned in three designs on the deadline. Two of them were slight variations of Cohne's design, which was very outdated, so I wont post those here.


As for the third option, well, it's the one you're looking at! They liked it so much that they asked to have it built, even though it was about 400% of the budget they had originally planned for. Needless to say my boss was pleased, and needless to say, I was very proud of myself!

Cascio & Farber

In my time at Saturno I was asked to use any down time I had to work on our catalog. I was encouraged to make sample websites that reflected the current trends in web and UX design, and this was one of them. While being one of my personal favorites, it was never selected by any of our clients and never went live. However, a working version can be found on my GitHub.


This website aimed to reflect the 2017 trends of typographic hero banners (as opposed to stock photos or videos), and parallax scrolling (you will see that the background is static, the text is in the forefront, but there are decorative text layers between the two as well, with their own scrolling speeds).

Glass Organics

In late 2016 I was hired to do a logo and some package design for a company that was selling Yaupon tea (the North American version of Mate, a highly caffinated tea leaf that grows in some southern states.


I wanted to make it look rural, historic and earthy, like Tazo, but also entirely American (unlike Tazo). To walk this line, I designed a stamp that was later used to print onto plain paper bags, giving it a colonial, "Boston tea party" look. A fun side effect of this was that I cut his packaging budget in half, as the stamps were cheap and the paper bags even more so.

Caroline Hanssen

Caroline Hanssen is a high end tutor in Marin, California. She hired me because she wanted a rebranding, business cards and a website that completely broke the mold. I was under instructions to not use apples, pencils, graduation caps, or any other symbol of education. I was also told to make a completely unusual and modern layout.


I settled on a cinemagraph owl. Mildly related to education, but mostly just quirky, original and ornate.
I built the site using Bootstrap, and you can view it here.
